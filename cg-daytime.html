Daytime Compression Garments

Daytime compression garments are designed to help control swelling and support circulation while you are upright and active. They are often used for patients with lymphedema, venous insufficiency, or chronic edema of the arms, legs, or trunk.

These garments provide a graduated level of compression, with the greatest support usually at the farthest part of the limb and slightly less as the garment moves closer to the body. This assists the natural movement of fluid back toward the central circulation throughout the day.

Great Lakes works with patients and providers to select appropriate garment styles, sizes, and materials based on limb measurements, diagnosis, activity level, and skin condition.

Fit, Use, and Care

Proper fit is essential for comfort and effectiveness. Garments are sized using detailed limb measurements, and in some cases custom-made options are used when standard sizes do not provide an appropriate fit.

Your referring therapist or provider will recommend how long to wear your daytime garments each day. Many patients put them on in the morning after swelling has decreased and remove them in the evening. Wearing instructions should always follow the guidance of your healthcare provider.

Who May Benefit from Daytime Garments

Daytime compression garments may be appropriate for patients who:

  • Have lymphedema or chronic swelling of an arm, leg, or trunk
  • Have venous insufficiency or varicose veins with edema
  • Are transitioning from intensive therapy to a home maintenance program
  • Need consistent daytime support to help maintain limb volume

The type, compression level, and wearing schedule of your garments are determined by your provider or therapist. Our role is to help make sure your garments fit correctly, feel as comfortable as possible, and support your long-term treatment goals.
