Pneumatic Compression After DVT

After a deep vein thrombosis (DVT) has been diagnosed and treated, pneumatic compression may be used to help manage chronic swelling, improve comfort, and reduce the risk of long-term complications such as post-thrombotic syndrome.

These devices are typically introduced once the treating provider confirms that it is safe to do so, taking into account the location of the clot, current medications, and your overall clinical status.

Great Lakes Medical supports patients and providers by supplying appropriate compression systems, training on home use, and assisting with documentation for coverage when available.

How the Device Works

A venous pneumatic compression device gently squeezes the limb in a controlled pattern to support blood flow back toward the heart. This can help reduce residual swelling, support comfort, and promote venous return after a DVT, when prescribed by your provider.

During your equipment visit, a Great Lakes representative will review how to apply the garment, operate the device, and follow the treatment schedule recommended by your physician or specialist.

Devices are intended to complement, not replace, medical therapies such as anticoagulation, lifestyle changes, and other interventions ordered by your care team.

Who May Benefit

Pneumatic compression after DVT may be considered for patients who:

  • Have a history of deep vein thrombosis in the leg
  • Experience ongoing swelling, heaviness, or discomfort
  • Are at risk for or have signs of post-thrombotic syndrome
  • Have been evaluated and cleared by the treating provider for compression

Compression therapy should always follow the recommendations of the healthcare team managing the DVT. Any sudden increase in swelling, pain, or shortness of breath should be treated as urgent and reported immediately.
