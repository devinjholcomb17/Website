Pneumatic Compression for Lymphedema

Pneumatic compression devices are designed to gently move fluid out of congested tissues and back into the circulatory system. For patients living with lymphedema, these devices can help reduce swelling, improve comfort, and support daily activities at home.

Treatment is typically ordered by your provider as part of a complete lymphedema management program that may also include compression garments, exercise, elevation, and skin care. The goal is to control swelling, protect the skin, and improve quality of life over time.

Great Lakes Medical works with patients, therapists, and physicians to select appropriate devices and garment styles based on limb involvement, severity of swelling, functional goals, and insurance guidelines.

How the Device Works

A lymphedema pneumatic compression device uses a garment with multiple air chambers that inflate and deflate in a specific sequence. This creates a gentle, wave-like motion that helps move lymphatic fluid from the limb toward the central circulation.

Your referring provider will prescribe the pressure settings and recommended treatment time. During a teaching visit, a Great Lakes representative will review how to position the garment, operate the device, and follow your prescribed routine safely at home.

When used consistently, these devices can support long-term volume reduction and help maintain the results of in-clinic therapy.

Who May Benefit

Pneumatic compression for lymphedema may be appropriate for patients who:

  • Have primary or secondary lymphedema of the arm, leg, or trunk
  • Experience persistent swelling despite conservative measures
  • Have completed or are actively engaged in lymphedema therapy
  • Need a home-based option to help maintain limb volume

Final decisions about device type, garment style, and treatment plan are made by your referring provider. Our role is to support that plan with appropriate equipment, education, and follow-up.
